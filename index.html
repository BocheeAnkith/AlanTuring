Alan Turing's Legacy

Alan Turing was a British mathematician and logistician who contributed to mathematics, cryptanalysis, logic, philosophy, and mathematical biology and also to the new areas later named computer science, cognitive science, artificial intelligence, and artificial life. He was educated in a private school and then got accepted into Cambridge University to study mathematics, where he wrote a paper on computable numbers and influenced computing worldwide. He eventually moved to Princeton University to study for a PHD in Mathematical Logic. He then created the concept of the Turing Machine which laid the groundwork for modern computing. Turing then joined the Government Code and Cypher School, and, at the outbreak of war with Germany, he moved to the organization’s wartime headquarters, where the opposing team had succeeded in deducing the internal wiring of Enigma (their code machine) and made a code-breaking machine they called the Bomba. However, Turing and others developed a code-breaking machine called Bombe, which supplied them with military intelligence, decoded tons of intercepted messages. Turing also devised the first systematic method for breaking messages called “Tunny”. Turing also designed the first complete specification of an electronic stored-program all-purpose digital computer. He also wrote the first-ever programming manual, and his programming system was used in the Ferranti Mark I, the first marketable electronic digital computer. Turing was a founding father of artificial intelligence and proposed the "Turing Test" as criteria for what an artificial computer is thinking.
